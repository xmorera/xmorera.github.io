AI Won't Take Your Job, Until Everyone Has AI. Then What? Will It?

Created on 2025-06-16 12:59

Published on 2025-06-17 16:00

"AI is going to take my job." We’ve all heard this fear. But a more accurate take is: "AI won’t take your job, but someone using AI will."

This phrase has become almost cliché in tech circles, and yet, it holds truth. Right now, AI is a multiplier—a tool that makes good professionals faster, sharp teams more productive, and companies more efficient.

But here’s a provocative thought: What happens when everyone uses AI?

What happens when access to tools becomes universal? When productivity levels out because everyone’s automation stack looks the same?

We’ll be back to square one—level ground. And that’s when the real differentiator returns: human creativity.

Not prompts. Not plugins. Not workflows. But the ability to see patterns where others see noise. To connect dots that haven't even been drawn yet. To imagine, explore, and build beyond the obvious.

AI can co-write, suggest, correct—but it can’t originate a vision. That’s still our domain.

It is still your responsibility. This is a double edge sword. If you rely too much on AI, you will get too lazy and get to a point where you don't create anything original.

That's why you need to look at AI as a tool that helps you, not as the "thing that creates my stuff"!

As we move into a future where AI becomes a commodity, it’s not just about how well you use the tools, but what you use them for. Creativity, empathy, strategic thinking—these will once again rise to the top.

For example, I am on a mission to democratize knowledge through AI and video—making learning faster, scalable, and more human. The "human" part may seem ironic, because I do it with AI-generated video. But it is my productivity which makes it stand out.

So yes, learn the tools. Master the workflows. But don’t forget to flex your most irreplaceable muscle: your imagination.

You are going to be the main differentiator. Make it count. Make a difference.
