How I Found My Mission in Life

Created on 2025-08-05 14:15

Published on 2025-08-05 14:29

Not everyone believes in destiny, but I do believe we all have a mission on this planet. Some people find it early. Others never do. I was lucky enough to stumble into mine — by accident — and it changed my life. I have about 400 thousand learners in Pluralsight to show. Here's my personal story.

About 20 years ago, I was working at a company called ArtinSoft (Artificial Intelligence Software, now part of Snowflake). They built a platform that converted code from one programming language to another — a big deal back then (and still kind of is). We had a contract with Microsoft to help them migrate from VB6 to VB.NET.

I had three things going for me:

  • I could code

  • I could explain what I was doing

  • I genuinely loved helping others

That made me a good fit for Microsoft's Route 64 program (Microsoft Learn, Intel Corporation and HP), where I traveled the world teaching Microsoft partners how to adopt 64-bit computing. We didn’t just show up and present — we built the labs, delivered the sessions, and answered the hard questions.

It was the beginning of something I didn’t know I’d love so much: teaching technology to people in a way that makes sense.

That passion carried through the rest of my career:

  • At Search Technologies, I built a search API for EY and led development on a massive patent processing system at CPA Global.

  • Along the way, I trained developers on Agile and Scrum almost every other week. (nod to the Scrum Alliance)

  • Eventually, I wrote to a company I’d heard of — Pluralsight — and said, “Hey, I’m already teaching this stuff. Want me to record it?”

That first course on Scrum and JIRA turned into another… and another… and now I’ve published over 50 courses for over 400,000 learners (just on Pluralsight alone).

I also worked for five years at Cloudera, and about seven years as a vendor for Microsoft.

And throughout all of that, I noticed something:

Training people is slow, expensive, and hard to scale.

Especially when it’s instructor-led:

  • Endless flights

  • Weeks away from home

  • Jet lag, bad food, and 80 extra pounds later… I knew something had to change

But that wasn’t the only issue.

Creating video training also has its problems:

  • Not everyone’s good at it

  • It takes forever to record and edit

  • Updates are a nightmare

  • Everyone does it differently, so quality is inconsistent

So I thought:

“What if we could take everything I’ve learned — as a developer, instructor, content creator, and problem-solver — and build a platform that made training 10x easier and faster?”

That’s why I created Lupo.ai. It is the result of tools that I've been creating for years for Pluralsight plus Cloudera's NGPS-inspired workflow.

Now, I know there are plenty of video AI platforms out there — Synthesia , Vyond, Colossyan, and others. Great tools, but here’s the thing:

I didn’t build a company that creates videos.

I built a platform that solves training. I am not doing edu-tainment. I enable AI-assisted education!

There’s a difference.

I did not found a company that creates videos.

I founded a company that solves training.

Lupo isn't just about video generation. It’s about accelerating knowledge transfer at scale — with:

  • Smart automation

  • AI-powered workflows

  • Enterprise-ready publishing tools

  • Localization, versioning, and updates that don’t suck

And it works because it was built by someone who’s lived the pain of training — in every format, industry, and continent.

This isn’t a startup story. It’s a mission. One that started with a line of VB6 and a room full of confused developers. And it keeps going.

If you’re trying to scale partner, customer, or employee training — I’ve probably faced the same problem you’re dealing with right now.

And I have a vision for the future where we have our own personal AI-tutor, but that's the meat and potatoes of a different edition.

Let's talk, exchange ideas, and help each other improve the world.

Let's ride this wave of AI into the future.
