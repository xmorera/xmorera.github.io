## Title
10 Things I Wish I Knew Before Building a B2B SaaS in a New Category

## Description
When you're creating a product in a category that doesn't fully exist yet, you're educating the market at the same time you're selling to it. This is exhausting and expensive in ways that building a better version of something familiar is not. Xavier has lived this with Lupo.AI — in 2019, "AI-generated training content" was not a recognized category. Here's what building in a new category actually teaches you.

## Key Points
1. **You'll spend half your sales calls explaining the category, not your product** — budget time and messaging accordingly
2. **The first customers are category believers, not product evaluators** — find people who already feel the pain acutely
3. **"No" often means "not yet"** — some prospects you lost in year 2 will come back in year 5 when the category is established
4. **Your pricing will be wrong** — new categories have no reference points; plan to iterate on price more than you expect
5. **Case studies are more valuable than features** — one well-documented win in a recognizable company does more than 20 feature releases
6. **Beware of "interesting" meetings** — enterprise prospects will spend hours with you out of curiosity, not purchase intent. Learn to qualify faster.
7. **Your vision will be right too early** — the market usually arrives 2-3 years after you expected
8. **Platform partners are your leverage** — in a new category, distributing through an established marketplace (Microsoft, Salesforce) borrows category credibility
9. **The category will be commoditized faster than you think** — build your moat in distribution and relationships, not just technology
10. **You need community around the category, not just the product** — LACademy, EO forums, industry groups become your category-building infrastructure
