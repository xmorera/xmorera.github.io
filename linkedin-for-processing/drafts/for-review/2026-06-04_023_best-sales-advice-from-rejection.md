## Title
The Best Sales Advice I Ever Got Came From a Customer Who Said No

## Description
A specific story about a rejection that changed how Xavier thinks about enterprise sales. Not the motivational "every no gets you closer to a yes" version — the specific insight that came from a customer explaining why they weren't buying, that turned out to be product strategy gold.

## Key Points
- The setup: a prospect who engaged deeply with Lupo.AI, went through a full demo cycle, and then said no
- The instinct: move on, find the next lead
- What Xavier did instead: asked for an honest conversation about why
- What he heard: not "your product doesn't work" but a specific use case gap, pricing concern, or integration friction that was holding them back
- The insight unlocked: that one conversation revealed a pattern — several other prospects had the same unstated objection, but none of them had been asked
- The lesson about enterprise sales: the objection they give you is rarely the real objection. The real one is harder to say.
- How this changed his approach: asking "what would need to be true for this to work?" instead of "are you interested?"
- The product-sales loop: some of Lupo.AI's most valuable features came from listening to rejections, not to fans
- CTA: "Have you ever had a rejection teach you something that a sale never would have? What did you do with it?"

## Source Insight
Xavier's sales experience across many customer conversations where objections and hesitations revealed real product/market insights. The honesty and specificity here will resonate strongly with B2B founders.

## Tone
Story-driven, honest, practical. Not generic "rejection is a gift" — specific and actionable. The founder-as-listener angle.
