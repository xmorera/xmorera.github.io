## Title
Running Taught Me More About Business Than Any MBA

## Description
The parallels between endurance sports and building a company are not a cliché — they're a lived reality. Pacing. Showing up when you don't feel like it. Breaking down big goals into sustainable steps. Recovery as part of the performance, not a sign of weakness. This post draws honest connections between Xavier's experience with running/fitness and the founder mindset that actually sustains a long-term build.

## Key Points
- Nobody runs a marathon by sprinting it — yet founders try to sprint company-building
- Pacing: in running, going too fast early kills your finish. In startups, burning out your team (or yourself) in year 1 kills year 3
- Showing up consistently: training is mostly showing up, not peak performances. Same with building a company.
- The wall: every marathon runner hits it around mile 20. Every founder hits a version of it — usually in year 2 or 3
- Recovery is not optional: rest days aren't laziness — they're how your body consolidates the gains. Same for the mind.
- What running builds: tolerance for discomfort, patience with slow progress, trust in the process even when results aren't visible yet
- Running in Costa Rica: the terrain, the humidity, the beauty — there's something specific about building and training in a place that reminds you what matters
- CTA: "What sport or physical practice has taught you something unexpected about work or business?"
