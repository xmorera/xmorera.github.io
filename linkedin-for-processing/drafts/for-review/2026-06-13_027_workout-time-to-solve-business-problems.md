## Title
My Best Business Decisions Happen During Workouts (Not at My Desk)

## Description
There's a well-documented phenomenon where the mind solves problems in motion that it can't solve while staring at a screen. Xavier's personal experience with this: specific business breakthroughs, product insights, and strategic pivots that came during a run, a walk, or at the gym — not in a meeting or in front of a whiteboard.

## Key Points
- The neuroscience background: physical movement increases blood flow to the prefrontal cortex and activates "default mode network" — the brain's background problem-solving system
- The phenomenon: you step away from the hard problem, your brain keeps working on it. The answer arrives during a jog.
- Xavier's examples: specific decisions or insights that came during physical activity (he should share a real one)
- The "shower thought" is a feature, not a bug — it's the brain doing its best work when you stop forcing it
- The practical implication: if you're stuck on a decision, going for a walk is not procrastination — it's a thinking strategy
- The desk-time illusion: many founders think they're working hard when they're staring at a laptop with diminishing returns. Their best thinking happens elsewhere.
- The design recommendation: when stuck on something important, block a 30-minute movement session instead of the next meeting
- The Costa Rica advantage: when your office is 20 minutes from a beach or a jungle trail, the barrier to "go think outside" is low
- CTA: "Where do you do your best thinking? What triggers the breakthrough moments for you?"

## Source Insight
Xavier's life in Costa Rica with its strong outdoors culture. The connection between movement and creative thinking is both scientifically grounded and personally resonant for him.

## Tone
Science-informed but personal. Not preachy. Shares a specific personal observation and explains the mechanism behind it. Relatable to any knowledge worker.
