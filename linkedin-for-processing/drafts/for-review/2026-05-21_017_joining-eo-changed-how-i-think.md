## Title
Joining EO Changed How I Think About Entrepreneurship

## Description
Xavier came to Entrepreneurs' Organization (EO) as a technical founder who had been building alone for years. What he found was a different kind of resource: not investment or customers, but a peer group of founders who had already been through the stages he was navigating. This post is honest about what Xavier expected from EO vs. what he actually got, and why the peer-learning model is underrated.

## Key Points
- What Xavier expected when joining EO: connections that would lead to deals/investment/customers
- What he found: something more valuable — peer founders who could say "I went through exactly this in year 3, here's what I did"
- The forum model: a small group of founders who meet regularly and share with radical honesty. Not a networking event — a peer advisory group.
- The specific value: when you're stuck on a problem that feels unique and isolating, finding out that 5 other experienced founders solved the same problem is clarifying and grounding
- The "founder as bottleneck" realization: he didn't have this language until an EO conversation helped him name what was happening
- LACademy: what it means to be building not just a company but a community — Xavier's involvement in EO's Latin American program shows the community side of his identity
- The intangible benefit: entrepreneurship is lonely. EO is the community that makes it less so.
- The call to other technical founders: if your instinct is "I don't have time for a community," that's probably when you need it most
- CTA: "Are you part of any founder community? What's been the most unexpected value you got from it?"

## Source Insight
From EO/LACademy calls (October 2025 orientation, LACademy committee calls, Claudia/EO call, Xavier-Jorge Zona Azul call). Xavier's EO involvement is a genuine and growing part of his identity as a founder.

## Tone
Genuine, warm, community-oriented. Not a promotional post for EO — an honest reflection on what community gives a founder that working alone can't.
