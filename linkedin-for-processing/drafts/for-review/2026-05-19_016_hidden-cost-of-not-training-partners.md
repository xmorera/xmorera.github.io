## Title
The Hidden Cost of Not Training Your Partners (Nobody Measures This)

## Description
Enterprise software companies pour resources into their direct sales motion and almost nothing into enabling their partner ecosystem. Yet for most of these companies, partners represent 30-70% of their eventual revenue. Untrained partners stall implementations, generate support tickets, misrepresent the product, and quietly churn. This post puts a number on the problem and explains why most companies don't see it.

## Key Points
- The invisible metric: partner implementation failure rate / partner-attributed churn is rarely tracked
- What happens with untrained partners: slow implementations, wrong use cases, frustrated customers, product misrepresentation
- The ripple effect: one poorly trained partner can generate 10x the support load of a well-trained one
- The typical response: a document library and a certification exam that nobody passes. This is not training.
- The ISV and channel partner angle: software companies in Microsoft's ecosystem (ISVs) face exactly this problem — they need to enable hundreds of partners to sell and deploy their product
- The math on partner training ROI: a well-trained partner reduces implementation time by weeks. Multiply by 100 partners.
- What good partner training looks like: short modules (under 10 minutes), role-specific (sales vs. technical vs. support), available in the partner's language, evergreen (automatically updated as the product changes)
- AI makes this achievable for companies that couldn't previously afford a full partner enablement program
- CTA: "If you work in an ecosystem/partner-heavy business, how are you handling partner training? What's broken?"
