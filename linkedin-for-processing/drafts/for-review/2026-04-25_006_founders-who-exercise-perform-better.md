## Title
Founders Who Exercise Perform Better. Here's Why Most Still Don't.

## Description
Not a motivational post — an analytical one. Why does exercising make founders more effective, yet most founders treat their health like a low-priority ticket in their backlog? This post explores the gap between knowing and doing, and offers the honest reason health gets deprioritized when you're building.

## Key Points
- The research is clear: exercise improves cognitive function, stress tolerance, decision quality, and emotional regulation — all critical for founders
- Yet founders, who understand ROI better than anyone, consistently under-invest in the one asset with the highest return: their own physiology
- The real reason it gets cut: it doesn't create a deliverable, and it doesn't put out a fire
- Urgency vs. importance: exercise is always "important" but never "urgent" — so it gets bumped
- The reframe that works: treat your workout like a board meeting — it's non-negotiable because it affects every other decision
- Living in Costa Rica (one of the world's Blue Zones, Nicoya Peninsula): what the locals know about longevity that most entrepreneurs ignore
- The compound effect: you don't feel the benefit of one workout. You feel the benefit of 200.
- CTA: "How do you protect your health time when the startup pulls in every direction?"
