## Title
From 3-Hour Zoom Recording to a 20-Minute Training Module: The Math That Changes Everything

## Description
Concrete, numbers-driven post about the economics of AI-powered training content. What used to take days (scripting, recording, editing, translating) now takes 20 minutes. This isn't a product pitch — it's a systems-thinking post about what happens when production time collapses: organizations can train faster, iterate content faster, reach more languages, and actually keep training current. The numbers tell the story.

## Key Points
- Old model: 1 hour of finished training content = 40-100 hours of production work (script, record, edit, narrate, translate, review)
- New model with AI: 1 hour of finished content = 4-8 hours, with translation included
- The multiplier: a team that could produce 2 courses a month can now produce 20
- But more important than speed: freshness. Training content that takes 3 months to produce is outdated before it ships. Content that takes 2 days stays current.
- The Accenture use case: intermediate drafts — they send subject matter experts a finished module that needs 15 minutes of review, not a 3-day recording session
- The human element doesn't go away — quality, strategy, and judgment still require people. But production is no longer the constraint.
- What this unlocks for smaller companies: training programs that were previously only possible for enterprises with large L&D budgets
- CTA: "How much time does your team spend on training content production vs. content strategy?"

## Source Insight
Directly from the Accenture conversations (5 minutes vs. several hours), the Carrie Missele call (transformation story), and the recurring demo pattern across all Lupo.AI sales calls.
