## Title
Building in Latin America Taught Me What Silicon Valley Never Could

## Description
A genuine perspective piece from a founder who is building globally from Costa Rica — not relocating to San Francisco to get taken seriously. The lessons from building in a market that Silicon Valley doesn't understand: relationships matter more, timelines are different, trust is built differently, and the problems being solved are real. This is not a complaint about Silicon Valley — it's an honest comparison of what you gain from staying rooted in your actual market.

## Key Points
- The default VC advice: "move to SF / NYC to build credibility." Xavier never did.
- What Costa Rica offers that SF doesn't: proximity to the actual problem (Latin American enterprises and their training challenges), bilingual fluency, lower burn rate, and a lifestyle that sustains longevity
- Building for LA enterprises is not "emerging market charity" — it's serving a sophisticated, growing market that US-centric companies chronically underserve
- Relationship dynamics: in Latin America, trust precedes transaction in a way that changes your sales process entirely
- The EO network in Latin America: LACademy, regional chapters — a founder community that moves at a different pace but goes deeper
- What Silicon Valley exports: funding, network effects, and urgency. What it sometimes misses: patience, relationship depth, and regional context
- The global product, local roots advantage: Lupo.AI serves companies in Latin America, the US, and beyond — but the perspective of building from the region gives it authenticity in that market
- The honest challenge: raising capital, building US-level credibility from a Latin American base — what's harder and what's surprisingly easier
- CTA: "Are you building outside the typical tech hubs? What's your experience been?"

## Source Insight
Xavier's lived experience as a Costa Rican founder, EO LACademy involvement, and multiple conversations about Latin American market dynamics (TradeX, Terumo, Claudia/EO call).

## Tone
Confident, specific, genuine pride in building from Costa Rica. Not defensive — proud and practical. This is a unique perspective in the tech conversation.
