## Title
I've Helped 430,000 People Learn Technical Skills. Here's What Actually Works.

## Description
From his years building courses at Pluralsight — which reached 430,000+ learners — Xavier has seen patterns at scale that almost nobody else has. This post distills the earned wisdom: what makes people actually learn, retain, and apply technical knowledge, vs. what makes them click through content they'll never remember.

## Key Points
- After 430K learners, the signal is clear: completion ≠ learning. Engagement ≠ retention.
- The biggest misconception in corporate training: if someone watched the video, they learned it. They didn't.
- What works: application immediately after learning (practice problems, hands-on scenarios within 24 hours)
- What fails: passive consumption of long-form content without retrieval practice
- Length is almost always wrong: most training content is 3-5x longer than it needs to be
- The multilingual insight: learners in a second language absorb 30-40% less information. This isn't a language problem — it's a training design problem that you can solve with better content production.
- The best courses at Pluralsight had: clear outcomes, short modules, demonstrations, and a strong practitioner voice (not academic)
- What this means for AI-era training: AI removes the production barrier, but the design principles remain the same — short, relevant, action-oriented
- CTA: "What's the most effective training you've ever experienced? What made it work?"
