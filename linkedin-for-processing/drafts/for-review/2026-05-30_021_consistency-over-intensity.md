## Title
Consistency Over Intensity: What 2+ Years of Daily Habits Actually Looks Like

## Description
The internet sells transformation stories. This post tells a different story: what boring, consistent effort looks like over a real multi-year period — the skipped days, the resets, the gradual progress that doesn't feel dramatic until you look back at where you started. An honest account of what sustainable self-improvement actually looks like for a working founder.

## Key Points
- The transformation promise: 30-day challenges, dramatic before/afters, radical overhauls
- The reality of 2+ years: mostly unremarkable days, occasional setbacks, gradual progress that feels invisible until it isn't
- The key insight: intensity gets attention; consistency gets results
- Concrete observation: a person who trains moderately 300 days a year beats the person who trains intensely for 30 days then burns out, every time
- The startup parallel: companies that ship small improvements consistently beat the ones that disappear for 6 months to build the "big release"
- What consistency requires: systems that survive bad days, reasonable defaults (not heroic effort), and the willingness to do a "minimum viable workout" rather than nothing
- The mental model shift: instead of "am I doing enough?" ask "am I still doing it?"
