## Title
Burnout Is a Product Decision, Not a Lifestyle Choice

## Description
Reframe of the burnout conversation: founders often treat burnout as a personal failure or a willpower problem. The reality is that burnout is often the result of product and business decisions — taking on the wrong customers, saying yes to every feature request, trying to be everything to everyone. When your business is designed wrong, your body is the first system to flag it. This post offers a systems lens on burnout rather than a self-care lens.

## Key Points
- The standard burnout narrative: you worked too hard, need more sleep, take a vacation — then get back to it
- The part nobody says: if the business design didn't change, you'll burn out again in 6 months
- Burnout as a signal: your nervous system is telling you that the current model is unsustainable
- The real sources: wrong customers (high-maintenance, low-value), scope creep (you said yes to everything), role confusion (doing 4 jobs because hiring was delayed)
- Xavier's version: when he was doing every sales call, every demo, every customer onboarding — the body knows before the spreadsheet does
- The product decision angle: clarity about what you build, who you serve, and what you don't do is also a health decision
- Saying no is a health strategy — not just a business strategy
- The recovery: not a vacation. A redesign of what the work looks like going forward.
- CTA: "Have you ever traced a burnout back to a business decision you made — rather than just a pace decision?"

## Source Insight
Xavier's experience running Lupo.AI and his honest acknowledgment (ISV call) of the personal cost of being the primary sales, support, and delivery person. This connects health to business systems design.

## Tone
Analytical, reframing, specific. Avoids the generic "wellness" framing. Treats founders as systems thinkers who will respond to a systems-level explanation of their own burnout.
