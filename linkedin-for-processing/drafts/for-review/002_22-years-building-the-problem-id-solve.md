## Title
I Spent 22 Years Building the Problem I'd Eventually Solve

## Description
Origin story post. Xavier's journey from training people at Artinsoft on Visual Basic.NET → Microsoft global training programs → 430,000+ learners at Pluralsight → founding Lupo.AI. The insight: he didn't go looking for a startup idea. He spent two decades in pain until the pain became undeniable. This is a powerful founder narrative about how genuine expertise creates genuine solutions — and how the best products come from people who have lived the problem.

## Key Points
- Started in the late 1990s training engineers at a software shop in Costa Rica
- Microsoft work: running global training programs across regions and languages
- Pluralsight: built courses consumed by 430,000+ learners — the scale taught him what actually works and what wastes time
- The moment the idea clicked: "I spent hours recording one video. There had to be a better way."
- The problem was never content — it was the bottleneck of production: recording, editing, narrating, translating
- Lupo.AI wasn't a pivot or a trend-chase — it was the logical endpoint of 22 years in the same problem space
- Founders who understand their market intimately build differently from those who study it externally
- CTA: "Have you ever realized you were building the solution to your own oldest problem?"

## Source Insight
From Derek call (March 2026) and multiple intro calls where Xavier consistently tells this origin story. His authority comes from lived experience, not credentials.

## Tone
Personal, honest, reflective. First-person narrative. Not a brag — a recognition that the path made sense in hindsight.
