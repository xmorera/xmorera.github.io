## Title
The Hardest Part of Scaling Isn't the Product. It's You.

## Description
Vulnerable post about the founder bottleneck — when you're so good at something (sales, demos, pitching) that you become the ceiling on your own growth. Xavier has lived this: he's the best person to demo Lupo.AI, so he demos it. Every call. And that means growth stops when his calendar is full. This is one of the most common and least-discussed challenges of early-stage B2B founders.

## Key Points
- The trap: you're great at selling your product because you built it and understand it better than anyone
- The result: you become the single point of failure for growth
- Every hour Xavier spends on a sales call is an hour not spent on strategy, product, or building systems that scale
- This is the "strength that becomes a weakness" founder story
- The realization (from the ISV call): "I can't scale if I'm in every demo"
- What has to change: process documentation, sales enablement, hiring or partnering with people who can carry the pitch
- The personal transition: from "best at doing" to "best at enabling others to do"
- The EO insight: talking to other founders who hit the same ceiling — you're not broken, you're at a common inflection point
- CTA: "How did you break through the founder-as-bottleneck problem? What worked?"

## Source Insight
Directly from the ISV Success call and Xavier's candid acknowledgment that he can't scale the business while doing every sales call personally. Also echoes themes from his EO network conversations.

## Tone
Honest, self-aware, vulnerability done right — specific and useful, not just "being vulnerable for engagement." Practical and relatable to B2B founders.
