## Title
Everyone's Creating Training Videos. Almost Nobody Is Watching Them.

## Description
The enterprise training paradox: companies invest heavily in video content that sits unwatched in LMS platforms. This isn't a content problem — it's a format, length, and relevance problem. Xavier has lived this from both sides: as a trainer who made content and as someone who watched completion rates. This post surfaces the uncomfortable truth that most training video investment is waste, and what the research + practice says about what actually gets consumed.

## Key Points
- The uncomfortable stat: average LMS course completion rates are under 20% in most enterprises
- The reason isn't motivation — it's the wrong format: 45-minute modules nobody asked for
- The 3-hour Zoom recording as "training" — this is still happening in 2026 and it's almost criminal
- What gets watched: short (3-10 minute), contextual, role-specific, available at the moment of need
- The multilingual dimension: a video in English is useless to your team in Mexico, Brazil, or Costa Rica if their English isn't strong enough for technical content
- AI changes the production economics: what used to require a studio and 3 days can now be done in 20 minutes
- When production is cheap and fast, you can make the short, role-specific content that actually gets watched
- This is not a technology story — it's a behavior science story that technology finally makes possible
- CTA: "What's the worst example of bad training content you've had to sit through?"

## Source Insight
Xavier's Accenture conversations, multiple onboarding calls (Mostafa Sayed), and the recurring theme across all sales calls: customers have tried video training before and it didn't work — usually because it was too long, too generic, or in the wrong language.
