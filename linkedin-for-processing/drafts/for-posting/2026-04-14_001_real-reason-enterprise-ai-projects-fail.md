## Title
The Real Reason Enterprise AI Projects Fail (It's Not the Technology)

## Description
Everyone blames hallucinations, model quality, or integration complexity when AI projects stall. The real killer is adoption — specifically, that nobody trained the people who were supposed to use it. This post reframes AI failure as a knowledge-transfer problem, not a technology problem. Xavier's angle is earned: 22 years watching brilliant software deployments die on the floor of an employee's laptop because nobody built onboarding that worked at scale.

## Key Points
- AI projects fail at the last mile: the human adoption step
- Companies spend 90% of their AI budget on the model and 0% on training the people who will use it
- Xavier's example: enterprise software companies sending 3-hour Zoom recordings as "documentation" — it doesn't stick
- The bottleneck is not intelligence — it's transfer of knowledge from the few who understand to the many who don't
- What actually works: short, contextual, role-specific training that meets people where they are
- The companies winning with AI internally are the ones who invested in training programs alongside the technology
- CTA: "What's been the biggest obstacle to AI adoption in your organization?"
