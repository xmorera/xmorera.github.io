# Most people make resolutions.
2026-01-01

Most people make resolutions.
Consultants need to make commitments.
Every year, we promise ourselves we will “be more consistent,” “get more clients,” or “finally fix our positioning.”
And then client work takes over, a few fires appear, and those resolutions quietly disappear.
But if your goal this year is to build a serious consulting practice, you cannot go into it half-hearted. A consulting business does not grow on hope. It grows on the commitments you keep, especially when things get busy.
Here are 10 commitments that can change your consulting practice this year:
1) I will treat lead generation as part of my daily routine.
Not something I do when things are slow, but a non-negotiable habit that keeps my pipeline healthy.
2) I will focus on selling outcomes, not hours.
Clients do not buy time. They buy specific business results I can help them achieve.
3) I will run my consulting practice like a real business.
Every week I will review my offers, numbers, and pipeline, and make decisions based on data, not feelings.
4) I will be selective about who I meet with.
I will protect my time and energy, and prioritize conversations with the right-fit clients.
5) I will keep my message simple and easy to understand.
I will be able to explain who I help and how I help them in one clear, repeatable sentence.
6) I will follow one sales process and keep improving it.
No more reinventing the wheel on every call. I will refine one process until it works reliably.
7) I will create systems for anything I do more than twice.
If it repeats, it gets documented. Systems will save me time and reduce avoidable mistakes.
8) I will take ownership of my calendar.
I will block time for deep work and for my life, and let client work fit around that, not the other way around.
9) I will capture and share the results I help clients create.
I will turn wins into case studies, stories, and proof that make it easier for new clients to say yes.
10) I will invest in guidance and support that shortens my learning curve.
I will stop trying to figure everything out alone and learn from people who have already done it.
If you follow through on even half of these, your business will look very different by this time next year.
Which of these commitments are you actually willing to make this year?
